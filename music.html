My current thoughts on AI

Click to expand

I love neural networks as a technology. I had to chance to study and work on it, and its capabilities to filter signals, compress data and approximate any function opened a wonderful world of new possibilities in my computer graphics job. I also love the parallels of this technology with the human brain, its abilities in the scientific domain (like protein folding) and the crazy philosophical questions ChatGPT raises about intelligence and consciousness.

On the other side, I really despise the cheap copies of Ghibli's style that OpenAI popularized and the awful christmas commercials of McDonald's and Coca-Cola. When used as the single tool to produce art, I think it kills most of the fun. Creating a complete music or artwork with only a few words of prompt means that 99% of the artistic choices are filled by the AI. And on the ethical part, the ability to copy a person's style sounds like pure theft, and the environmental impact is catastrophic.

I would personally vote for a complete ban on fully AI-generated art.
I think there could be awesome creative tools to do with generative AI. A few lines of prompt is just not the way to go. It's a good interface for conversational agents like ChatGPT, but artistic tools should give more control and involvement to the artist.

Now let's talk about my personal art.
I did use a bit of generative AI here and there. Four of the last songs I made have a cover artwork partly generated by AI. I make zero money from my music and have very few listeners, so I'm used to copy-paste artworksfrom Pinterest of Google Images. This is not great, and I'm considering other ways as I'm getting more listeners recently.
For the tracks MADRUGADA, HAHAHA and NO MEIO PANCADÃO, I found the base of the artwork on Pinterest, which is entirely flooded with AI now. I did some edits on top of that with GIMP (copping, color grading, text, effects...). And for the artwork of the track Quebra No Funk, I commissioned a guy on Fiverr who used AI, although he did bring his own touch with Photoshop.
I'm also considering experimenting with AI-generated samples, but I don't like the idea of giving money to companies like Suno. My mind is not made up yet.

TL;DR: I like neural networks but I dislike fully AI-generated art.

Some thoughts about Brazilian Phonk

I recently discovered the "Brazilian Phonk" sub-genre, which mix recent phonk saturation and synth with baile funk's vocals and rythm. I completely fell in love with it. And I'm not te only one, it's getting super popular on Tiktok and Spotify.
The first contact is often made through gymbro content, and yes it's a perfect match. That distorded aggressivity, cryptic darkness, always mixed with a bit of fun and dance. I was getting tired of my metal workout playlist, and phonk brought back the hype.
However, what made me really addicted to it was the insane creativity this sub-genre has unveiled (especially with its derivatives like Bounce Funk, Krushfunk, Space Funk, etc.). There are so much young crazy-talented producers, and they produce so fast that it grows exponentially. One part of me loves seeing this jungle teeming with creativity, boosted by tiktok and lacking any boundary, wether it's classic musical rules or even ethics. They sample anything, go crazy on the effects, reupload slowed-down versions, generate AI vocals and covers... I really feel like everything is possible, and only music (as in, frequency coming to my ears) matters.
The other part of me is not a big fan of this algo-greedy culture...
